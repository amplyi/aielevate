---

The strongest EDMP business case is actually NOT AI, NOT memory, and NOT technology.

The business case is:

> Organizations preserve data, documents, workflows, tickets, emails, dashboards,
> AI outputs, approvals, and transactions.
>
> They do NOT preserve how important decisions were formed.

That gap becomes exponentially more expensive as AI adoption increases.

---

# The Executive Problem

Today an executive can answer:

* What happened?
* Who approved it?
* What was implemented?
* What was the outcome?

But they often cannot answer:

* Why was this decision made?
* What alternatives were considered?
* Which assumptions were used?
* Which risks were accepted?
* What signal triggered the decision?
* What changed between version 1 and version 5?

The organization remembers the outcome.

It forgets the reasoning.

---

# The Cost of Forgetting

Consider a €100M company.

Three years later:

* CFO leaves
* Program manager leaves
* AI model changes
* Strategy changes
* Board changes

The organization still has:

* PowerPoints
* Jira tickets
* Teams chats
* Email archives
* SharePoint folders

But nobody can reconstruct:

    Signal
    → Reasoning
    → Decision
    → Execution
    → Outcome

The institutional memory is broken.

---

# Why AI Makes This Worse

Before AI:

    Human
    → Decision

After AI:

    Human
    → AI
    → Human
    → Workflow
    → Approval
    → Decision

Decision velocity increases.

Decision traceability decreases.

Organizations start making more decisions faster while understanding less about
how those decisions were formed.

That is the paradox.

# EDMP's Core Value

    Institutional Memory

Instead of storing only:

EDMP stores:

    Why it happened
